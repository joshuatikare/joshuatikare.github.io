The Problem

Why This Talk
Matters Now

42% of Gen Z students report experiencing anxiety or depression. In a world where social media amplifies comparison and fear of failure, young people need to hear a different message.

Students today are paralyzed by perfectionism. They see success stories on Instagram but never see the failures behind them. They're told to "find their passion" and "be the best" without understanding that greatness comes from repeatedly failing and showing up anyway.

My talk directly addresses this crisis by reframing failure not as something to avoid, but as evidence that you're trying enough, taking enough risks, and building the resilience that leads to extraordinary outcomes.

About Joshua

From West End
to World Stage

I'm not a motivational speaker who talks about resilience from a distance—I'm a Stanford senior still navigating rejection every single day.

At age 8, I became the youngest performer in my role in Matilda the Musical on London's West End. But what audiences didn't see: the 300+ auditions, the 20 rejections for every callback, the voice nodules that nearly ended my career at age 10.

By my teens, I was competing internationally for England in field hockey—but only after choosing the unconventional path, picking a less popular sport, and fighting for every opportunity.

Today, as the first student from my school to attend Stanford, I'm studying Economics and Chinese while building a startup, releasing music, and inspiring the next generation.

The Talk

Twenty No's
for Every Yes

A powerful narrative journey through rejection, resilience, and the courage to pursue unconventional paths.

Act I

The Dancing Kid

Opens with me as a 4-year-old, dancing alone by the speaker. My parents encourage exploration. This sets the foundation: trying many things—and failing at most—was celebrated.

Act II

The 20:1 Ratio

Specific, vulnerable stories of rejection. Matilda auditions. Voice nodules. Hockey tryouts. Stanford vs. Oxford. Each failure teaching a lesson.

Act III

Still In The Arena

Ends with me today: building a startup, releasing music. I'm not preaching from a mountaintop—I'm still climbing, still failing, still trying.

"Successful people don't succeed despite trying many things—they succeed because they try many things."

Core message from the talk

Impact

What Students
Will Learn

01

Reframe Rejection

Learn to see "no" as evidence of trying, not a reflection of worth. Students understand that successful people experience more rejection because they attempt more opportunities.

02

Build Failure Resilience

Develop practical strategies for bouncing back faster. I share the exact mindset shifts that helped me audition for role #301 after 300 rejections.

03

Choose Your Path

Gain confidence to pursue unconventional paths over expected ones. Learn how to make decisions based on personal growth rather than external validation.

04

Embrace Multiple Passions

Discover why pursuing diverse interests builds unique advantages. Students learn that being a "jack of all trades" creates unexpected opportunities.
