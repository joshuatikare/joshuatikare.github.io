About

From West End to World Stage

Joshua Tikare embodies the power of perseverance and the courage to pursue unconventional paths.

At age 8, he became the youngest performer in his role in Matilda the Musical on London's West End. By his teens, he was competing internationally for England in field hockey. Today, as a Stanford University senior studying Economics and Chinese, he's building a startup, releasing music, and inspiring the next generation.

His journey demonstrates a fundamental truth: your relationship with failure determines your capacity for extraordinary achievement.

For every role Joshua landed, he auditioned for twenty he didn't get. For every goal scored, there were countless missed shots. For every success, there were failures that taught him resilience.

Speaking

Twenty No's for Every Yes

A powerful talk about resilience, failure, and the courage to pursue multiple passions.

Through personal stories of rejection in acting, sports, and academics, Joshua teaches students that successful people don't succeed despite trying many things—they succeed because they try many things.

What Students Will Learn

  • How to reframe rejection as a stepping stone rather than a roadblock
  • Why pursuing multiple passions builds resilience and creates unique opportunities
  • That successful people fail more often because they try more things
  • Practical strategies for choosing their own path over societal expectations
